## Protein Science

### Why 2.0 g/kg (140g/day)
- ISSN & ACSM recommend 1.6–2.2 g/kg/day for exercising individuals
- 2024 RCT showed 1.6 g/kg superior to 0.8 g/kg for muscle mass gains in older adults (Bagheri et al.)
- At 48, higher end counteracts age-related **anabolic resistance** (impaired MPS response to protein)

### Leucine Threshold (Critical for Age 40+)
- Older adults need ~2.8–3.0g leucine per meal to trigger muscle protein synthesis (MPS)
- Translates to **30–40g of high-quality protein per meal**
- Below threshold = no MPS signal fires
- Source: Zaromskyte et al., 2021; PMC 2023

### Protein Distribution
- MPS was 25% higher when protein distributed evenly across meals vs. skewed (Mamerow et al., 2014)
- Target: ~35g per meal × 4 eating occasions
- MPS peaks within 30 min of eating, stays elevated ~3 hours
- Space meals 3–5 hours apart

### Anabolic Window: Debunked
- Meta-analysis of 65 RCTs: timing had no effect on muscle growth (Schoenfeld et al., 2013)
- Enhanced anabolic state from training persists 48–72 hours
- Total daily protein matters, not timing relative to workout

## Hydration

- **Daily baseline:** ~100 oz (3L)
- **Pre-training:** 16–24 oz in the 2 hours before exercise
- **During training:** 6–12 oz every 15–20 minutes
- **Post-training:** 16–24 oz for every pound of sweat lost
- Dehydration reduces MPS, increases cortisol (competes with testosterone), and impairs multi-rep performance

## Supplements (Evidence-Based Only)

### Creatine Monohydrate — Strong Evidence
- **Dose:** 3–5g per day, every day (no loading necessary)
- Most studied supplement in sports nutrition
- Enhances resistance exercise performance via phosphocreatine energy system
- Meta-analyses: increases muscle thickness in 8–12+ week interventions with resistance training
- Safe for healthy adults — no evidence of kidney damage (ISSN; Candow et al., 2022)

### Vitamin D — Conditional
- **Dose:** 800–2,000 IU/day (if deficient; get blood test first)
- Important for muscle and bone function if serum level < 30 ng/mL
- Once sufficient (>50 nmol/L), additional supplementation = no further muscle benefit
- Source: PMC, 2018

### Omega-3 Fatty Acids (EPA/DHA) — Moderate Evidence
- **Dose:** 1–3g combined EPA+DHA per day
- Reduce inflammation, aid recovery
- Facilitators of training adaptation, not direct drivers of muscle growth
- Get from fatty fish (2–3x/week) first; supplement if dietary intake is low
- Source: PMC, 2018; 2020

### Whey/Casein Protein — Food, Not Supplement
- Whey: 20–40g stimulates MPS in older adults. Convenient when whole food meals fall short.
- Casein before bed: stimulates overnight protein synthesis in both young and elderly men
- Use as tools to hit 140g target, not requirements

## Common Myths Debunked

1. **"Eat within 30 min or lose gains"** — Anabolic window debunked. 65 RCTs: timing had no effect. (Schoenfeld, 2013)
2. **"High protein damages kidneys"** — Safe at 1.6–2.2 g/kg/day in healthy adults. (ISSN)
3. **"Body can only absorb 30g per meal"** — All protein is absorbed. 30g is MPS cap in young adults; older adults may need 40g+. (Schoenfeld & Aragon, 2018)
4. **"6 meals/day boosts metabolism"** — No metabolic advantage. 3–4 meals optimal for protein distribution. (ISSN)
5. **"Carbs make you fat"** — Calories make you fat. Carbs are protein-sparing and fuel training. (Frontiers in Nutrition, 2024)
