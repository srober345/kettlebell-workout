Progressive Overload Plan

How to keep getting stronger over time

12-Week Progression Cycle

Since kettlebells come in fixed weights, progression happens through volume, density, and load

Weeks 1-4: Adapt to New Order

  • Follow the program as written
  • Get used to the new exercise sequence (it will feel different)
  • The ballistic-first approach will feel harder initially because you're doing swings, high pulls, and golf swings when you're fresh instead of already fatigued
  • Focus on form and getting comfortable with the set structures (10x10, 5x20, 4x25)

Weeks 5-8: Increase Density

  • Monday: shorten rest by 5-10 seconds between sets
  • Wednesday: try to finish 5 minutes faster than week 1
  • Friday: step up one bell size on 2-3 exercises
  • Track your total session time — density improvement is real progress

Weeks 9-12: Increase Load

  • Start using a heavier bell for swings, rows, and presses across ALL days
  • Wednesday: introduce the superset option (see below) for exercises 4-9
  • Friday: use your heaviest available bell for exercises 1-6
  • If you've bought the 35 lb bell, start integrating it for swings

Every 5th Week: Deload

  • Use one bell size lighter across the board
  • Standard pace (Monday style) for all 3 days
  • Cut to 75 reps per exercise instead of 100 (3x25)
  • Focus purely on form and recovery
  • This is not laziness — it's science. Deloading prevents overtraining and allows adaptation.

Superset Option (Advanced)

Try after 4 weeks — pair exercises 4-9 to save time and increase metabolic demand

A superset means doing one set of Exercise A, immediately followed by one set of Exercise B, then resting. This works because you alternate push/pull muscles — while one recovers, the other works.

Superset Exercise A Exercise B Sets Rest After Both
SS1 Rows (20 reps) Push Outs (20 reps) 5 30 sec
SS2 Low Chest Flys (25 reps) Overhead Press (20 reps) 4-5 30 sec
SS3 Bicep Curls + Press (25 reps) Tricep Extensions (25 reps) 4 30 sec

This cuts ~10 minutes off the session while maintaining the same volume and actually increasing the metabolic demand (more work in less time = more calories burned).

Equipment Recommendation

The single best investment to maximize your results

You Need a 35 lb (16 kg) Kettlebell

Your current range tops out at 25 lbs. For a healthy adult male doing two-hand swings, research protocols typically use 16-24 kg (35-53 lbs). The swing is a hip-dominant ballistic movement — your hips and glutes are far stronger than 25 lbs.

Purchase Priority:

  • 1st: 35 lb (16 kg) — for swings, rows, farmer's carries
  • 2nd: 53 lb (24 kg) — when 35 lb swings feel routine (8-12 weeks). Standard men's training bell.

A quality cast-iron kettlebell costs $40-80 and will last a lifetime.

Why the 3-Day Variation Works

Day Training Stimulus What It Develops How It Feels
Monday Balanced sets, moderate pace Baseline strength-endurance, movement quality Solid work — challenging but sustainable
Wednesday Short rest, high density Cardiovascular conditioning, calorie burn, mental toughness Breathless — HR stays elevated the entire session
Friday Heavier bells, more rest Muscular strength, muscle tone, power output Heavy — muscles burn, each rep feels demanding

Together, the three days hit ALL of your goals:

Endurance ← Wednesday | Conditioning ← Wednesday + high-rep structure | Toned Body ← Friday + Monday
