Your 12 Exercises — Resequenced, Restructured & Maximized

Schedule: Mon / Wed / Fri
Duration: 50-70 min
Exercises: 12 per session
Total Reps: 1,200 per session

Why This Program Works

Built on peer-reviewed research from ACE, NSCA, StrongFirst, and multiple published studies

1

Ballistic Exercises First

Power movements when your central nervous system is freshest. Each swing rep produces more force, burns more calories, and builds more power. (McGill & Marshall, 2012)

2

Compound Before Isolation

Large muscle groups first while they're fresh, small muscle groups last when they're pre-warmed from compound movements.

3

Push/Pull Alternation

Working opposing muscle groups back-to-back reduces local fatigue, so you maintain rep quality deeper into the workout.

4

Optimized Set Structure

10x10 for ballistic moves keeps every rep explosive. 5x20 for compounds maintains form. 4x25 for isolation work targets endurance.

5

Periodized Across 3 Days

Research (PMC9022701, 2022) proved periodized training produces statistically significant endurance gains while identical sessions did not.

The Optimized Exercise Order

Your new permanent sequence — science behind every placement

# Exercise Phase Why This Position
1 Kettlebell Swings Power The #1 KB exercise. Highest CNS demand, full posterior chain. Must be first when you're freshest.
2 High Pulls Power Second ballistic movement. Traps, rear delts, biceps — vertical pulling power while CNS is still fresh.
3 Side (Golf) Swings Power Third ballistic movement. Rotational power through core and hips. Benefits from fresh core stabilizers.
4 Rows Compound Pull Horizontal pull — lats, rhomboids, rear delts, biceps. Placed after ballistics, before pressing.
5 Push Outs Compound Push Horizontal push — chest, front delts, triceps. Push AFTER pull = agonist-antagonist alternation.
6 Overhead Press Compound Push Vertical push — shoulders, triceps. Pressing muscles are warm but not exhausted.
7 Low Chest Flys Accessory Front delt / chest isolation. Bridges compound to isolation work.
8 Bicep Curls + Press Accessory Full arm chain compound. Placed after heavy pulling pre-warmed biceps.
9 Tricep Extensions Isolation MUST come after all pressing — pressing pre-fatigues triceps for deeper isolation.
10 Side Extensions Core Oblique/lateral core. Near the end so you don't pre-fatigue stabilizers needed earlier.
11 Waist Halos Mobility Low-intensity core + hip mobility. Active recovery after heavy work.
12 Head Halos Mobility Shoulder mobility + trap activation. Lowest intensity goes last.

Weekly Overview

Same 12 exercises, three different execution styles

Standard Day

Balanced baseline workout. Solid effort across all 12 exercises.

  • 10x10 / 5x20 / 4x25 set structure
  • HR rest: 140-145 bpm (~30 sec)
  • 55-65 minutes

Density Day

Maximum metabolic burn. Faster pace, shorter rest.

  • 5x20 / 4x25 / 2x50 set structure
  • Short rest: 15-20 sec (HR stays 150+)
  • 45-55 minutes

Friday

Power Day

Maximum force per rep. Heavier bells, more rest.

  • 10x10 / 5x20 set structure
  • Full rest: 45-60 sec (HR to 130-135)
