12-Week Progression Plan

Double Progression — the most effective overload strategy for dumbbells

How Double Progression Works

Research shows adding reps at fixed weight produces equal gains to adding weight alone

1

Start at Bottom of Rep Range

Begin each exercise at the prescribed weight and the lowest rep count. Example: Incline Press at 25 lb x 8 reps.

2

Add Reps Over Weeks

Each week, try to add 1-2 reps per set at the same weight. When you can complete the top of the rep range for all sets, you're ready to progress.

3

Increase Weight, Reset Reps

Move up one dumbbell increment (usually 5 lbs) and drop back to the bottom of the rep range. Repeat the cycle.

Day Type Rep Range Example Progression
Strength 6 → 8 reps 25 lb x 6 → 25 lb x 7 → 25 lb x 8 → 30 lb x 6
Hypertrophy 10 → 12 reps 20 lb x 10 → 20 lb x 11 → 20 lb x 12 → 25 lb x 10
Metabolic 12 → 15 reps 15 lb x 12 → 15 lb x 14 → 15 lb x 15 → 20 lb x 12

12-Week Timeline

Weeks 1-4: Foundation

  • Learn all 14 movements with proper form and tempo
  • Start at the bottom of each rep range with conservative weights
  • Focus on tempo — 3-sec eccentrics on Strength day, pauses on Hypertrophy day
  • Master the superset and circuit flow on Hypertrophy and Metabolic days
  • Log every weight used — this is your baseline

Weeks 5-8: Increase Density

  • Add 1-2 reps per set on exercises where you can maintain form
  • Reduce rest periods by 10-15 seconds per set
  • Strength day: aim to hit top of rep range (8 reps) on all exercises
  • Metabolic day: try to complete circuits 2-3 minutes faster than Week 1
  • If you can hit all target reps easily, you're ready for heavier weights

Weeks 9-12: Increase Load

  • Move up one DB increment (5 lbs) on 2-3 exercises per session
  • Reset to bottom of rep range at the new weight
  • Prioritize increasing weight on compounds first (presses, rows, squats)
  • Keep isolation exercises at current weight if form would suffer
  • 4-second eccentrics on Strength day for maximum tension at heavier loads

Every 5th Week: Deload

  • Drop weight by 30% across all exercises
  • Reduce to 2 sets per exercise (instead of 3-4)
  • Keep all three day types but at reduced intensity
  • Focus on perfect form and movement quality
  • This is NOT a week off — it's strategic recovery for continued gains

When You Can't Add Weight

With a 5-30 lb range, you'll eventually max out. These strategies keep you progressing.

Strategy How It Works When to Use
Slow the Eccentric Move from 2-sec to 4-sec lowering phase. Increases time under tension without needing more weight. When you can hit 8+ reps at max weight on Strength day
Add Pauses 1-3 second pause at the most difficult position (bottom of press, top of row). Eliminates momentum. When exercises feel "easy" at current weight
Go Unilateral Two-arm row → one-arm row. Goblet squat → Bulgarian split squat. Doubles effective load per limb. When bilateral exercises are maxed out
Increase Range of Motion Deficit lunges (front foot elevated). Deeper squats. Floor press to full DB press. When full ROM at current weight is easy
Add Sets Go from 3 sets to 4 on an exercise. Research shows volume is the primary driver of hypertrophy. When reps are maxed and weight can't increase
Reduce Rest Same work in less time = greater metabolic stress. Cut rest by 15 seconds per round. Metabolic day — try to beat your time each week

Tempo Guide

Tempo notation: Eccentric — Pause — Concentric (in seconds)

Day Tempo Meaning Time Under Tension / Set
Strength 3-1-1 3 sec down, 1 sec pause at bottom, 1 sec up ~40 sec (8 reps x 5 sec)
Hypertrophy 2-1-1 2 sec down, 1 sec pause at stretch, 1 sec up ~48 sec (12 reps x 4 sec)
Metabolic 2-0-1 2 sec down, no pause, 1 sec up ~45 sec (15 reps x 3 sec)

Research sweet spot for hypertrophy: 30-60 seconds time under tension per set. All three day types fall within this range.

Equipment Guide

Hotel Gym Essentials

This program is designed for the typical hotel gym setup:

  • 1. Dumbbell rack (5-50 lb in 5 lb increments)
  • 2. Adjustable bench (flat + incline)
  • 3. Open floor space (for farmer's walks and lunges)

No bench available? Substitute floor press for flat press, and floor fly already uses the floor. For incline curl, sit on the edge of a chair with back support.

Why the 3-Day Variation Works

Day Training Stimulus What It Develops How It Feels
Strength (M) Heavy loads, long rest, slow eccentrics Neural adaptation, maximal force, bone density Heavy and deliberate — you should feel strong
Hypertrophy (W) Moderate loads, supersets, metabolic stress Muscle growth, definition, work capacity Pumped and fatigued — muscles should feel full
Metabolic (F) Lighter loads, circuits, minimal rest Fat burning, EPOC, cardiovascular fitness Breathless and sweating — heart rate stays elevated
