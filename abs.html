Core Training

Abdominal Routine

Schedule: Tue / Thu / Sat
Exercises: 8 total
Duration: 15-20 min per session
Equipment: Bodyweight + 1 kettlebell (10-15 lb)

Why This Works

Built on EMG research, McGill's spine biomechanics, and progressive core training principles

1

Stability Before Movement

Deep stabilizers (TvA) activated first via dead bugs and planks. McGill research shows fatigue-free stabilizers prevent injury and improve force transfer.

2

EMG-Ranked Exercises

Every exercise selected from peer-reviewed EMG studies. Bicycle crunch = 248% activation vs traditional crunch (ACE/SDSU Study, 2001).

3

Full Core Coverage

8 exercises systematically target all 6 core regions: upper RA, lower RA, external obliques, internal obliques, transverse abdominis, and anti-rotation.

4

3-Day Rotation

Each session emphasizes a different movement pattern: stability+flexion, lateral+rotation, strength+isometric. Progressive overload every 4-6 weeks.

The 8 Exercises

Ordered by research — stability first, dynamic last

# Exercise Phase Why This Position
1 Dead Bug (KB) Stability Deep stabilizer activation first — TvA "switches on" before any dynamic work (McGill)
2 Reverse Crunch Flexion Lower RA targeting with minimal hip flexor involvement. Spine-safe pelvic tilt pattern.
3 Bicycle Crunch Flexion Highest EMG activation of any ab exercise — 248% of standard crunch (ACE Study)
4 Plank Shoulder Taps Anti-Rotation Anti-rotation stability demands a fresh core. Full RA + oblique co-contraction.
5 Side Plank Hip Dips Rotation Dynamic side bridge targets obliques + TvA. Builds on McGill's recommended side plank.
6 KB Russian Twist Rotation Loaded rotational power. Placed after anti-rotation to pre-activate oblique stabilizers.
7 Hollow Body Hold (KB) Isometric Full RA isometric under load. Anti-extension challenge for the entire anterior chain.
8 Lying Leg Raises Flexion The ONLY exercise ranking high across ALL 4 major EMG studies. Lower RA + hip flexor strength.

Workout A — Tuesday

Focus: Stability + Flexion
Time: ~18 min
Principle: Stability first → dynamic flexion last
# Exercise Sets x Reps Rest Notes
1 Dead Bug (KB) 3 x 10/side 45 sec Stability first — hold KB overhead, extend opposite arm/leg. Keep lower back pressed to floor.
2 Reverse Crunch 3 x 15 45 sec Slow pelvic tilt — lift hips off floor by curling pelvis toward ribcage. No momentum.
3 Bicycle Crunch 3 x 20 total 45 sec SLOW tempo (3 sec per rep). Elbow to opposite knee. Full rotation each rep.

Workout B — Thursday

Focus: Lateral + Rotation
Time: ~18 min
Principle: Anti-rotation first → loaded rotation last
# Exercise Sets x Reps Rest Notes
1 Plank Shoulder Taps 3 x 10/side 45 sec High plank position. Tap opposite shoulder without rotating hips. Fight the twist.
2 Side Plank Hip Dips 3 x 12/side 45 sec Side plank on forearm. Dip hip to floor, drive back up. Slow and controlled.
3 KB Russian Twist 3 x 12/side 45 sec Feet off floor, lean back 45°. Rotate KB side to side. Touch floor each side.

Workout C — Saturday

Focus: Strength + Isometric
Time: ~15 min
Principle: Maximum tension — loaded isometric + controlled flexion
# Exercise Sets x Reps Rest Notes
1 Hollow Body Hold (KB) 3 x 20-30 sec 60 sec Hold KB overhead, legs extended 6 inches off floor. Arms and legs stay straight. Squeeze everything.
2 Lying Leg Raises 3 x 12 60 sec Hands under hips. Lower legs slowly (3 sec down). Don't let lower back arch off floor.
24 working sets per week — 8 exercises across 3 sessions

Exercise Guide

Form cues and illustrations for all 8 abdominal exercises

1

Dead Bug (KB)

Stability

Form Cues

  • Lie on your back, arms extended toward ceiling holding KB (10-15 lb)
  • Knees bent 90 degrees — shins parallel to floor
  • Press your lower back INTO the floor — no gap. This is the key.
  • Slowly extend opposite arm and leg until nearly straight
  • Return to start, then switch sides. That's one rep per side.
  • If your back arches off the floor, you've gone too far — reduce range
2

Reverse Crunch

Flexion

Form Cues

  • Lie on back, arms at sides or hands under hips for support
  • Start with knees bent, feet off the floor
  • Curl your pelvis toward your ribcage — lift hips off the floor
  • Think "roll the pelvis up" — don't kick your legs
  • Lower slowly — take 2-3 seconds to return
  • Keep the movement small and controlled — quality over range
3

Bicycle Crunch

Flexion

Form Cues

  • Lie on back, hands lightly behind your head (don't pull your neck)
  • Lift shoulders off the floor and hold
  • Bring right elbow toward left knee while extending right leg
  • Rotate your TORSO — don't just move your elbow
  • Slow tempo: 3 seconds per rep minimum
  • Full rotation each rep — feel the obliques squeeze
4

Plank Shoulder Taps

Anti-Rotation

Form Cues

  • Start in high plank — hands under shoulders, body straight
  • Feet shoulder-width apart for stability (narrow = harder)
  • Lift one hand and tap the opposite shoulder
  • The goal: keep your hips perfectly still. Don't rock side to side.
  • If hips rotate, widen your feet or slow down
  • Brace your core like someone is about to poke your stomach
5

Side Plank Hip Dips

Rotation

Form Cues

  • Side plank on your forearm, elbow directly under shoulder
  • Stack your feet (or stagger for easier balance)
  • Drop your hip toward the floor in a controlled dip
  • Drive your hip back up to the straight-body position
  • Keep your top hand on your hip or extend to ceiling
  • Don't let your body rotate forward or backward
6

KB Russian Twist

Rotation

Form Cues

  • Sit with knees bent, lean back to ~45 degrees
  • Lift feet off the floor (easier: keep heels on floor)
  • Hold KB at chest level with both hands
  • Rotate your torso and touch the KB to the floor on each side
  • Rotate from the WAIST — don't just move your arms
  • Slow and controlled — each rep should take 2-3 seconds
7

Hollow Body Hold (KB)

Isometric

Form Cues

  • Lie on your back, arms extended overhead holding KB
  • Raise legs 6 inches off the floor, keep them straight
  • Raise shoulder blades off the floor, arms stay straight
  • Your body should form a slight "banana" or crescent shape
  • Press your lower back into the floor — no arch
  • Breathe steadily — don't hold your breath
8

Lying Leg Raises

Flexion

Form Cues

  • Lie on your back, hands under your hips (palms down) for support
  • Raise both legs to vertical (90 degrees)
  • Lower your legs SLOWLY — take 3 full seconds
  • Stop when legs are 6 inches from the floor
  • If your lower back arches, don't go as low
  • Raise back to vertical to start the next rep

Progression Plan

How to progress the abdominal routine over time

Weeks 1-2: Learn the Movements

  • 2 sets per exercise, focus on form and controlled tempo
  • Master the breathing pattern for each movement
  • Prioritize slow negatives and full range of motion
  • Use bodyweight only for Dead Bug and Hollow Body (no KB yet)

Weeks 3-4: Build Volume

  • Move to 3 sets per exercise, begin adding reps
  • Add KB to Dead Bug and Hollow Body Hold (10 lb)
  • Increase Bicycle Crunch to 24 reps, Lying Leg Raises to 15 reps
  • Reduce rest to 30-40 seconds if form stays solid

Weeks 5-6: Add Load

  • 3 sets, add load (heavier KB for Dead Bug, Hollow Body, Russian Twist)
  • Progress from 10 lb to 15 lb KB on loaded exercises
  • Increase hold time on Hollow Body to 30-40 seconds
  • Narrow foot stance on Plank Shoulder Taps for more challenge

Week 7: Deload & Reset

  • Deload — 2 sets, lighter load. Then swap 2-3 exercises for next cycle
  • Focus on perfect form and controlled tempo
  • Evaluate which exercises feel strongest and which need more work
  • Plan the next 6-week cycle with exercise swaps
